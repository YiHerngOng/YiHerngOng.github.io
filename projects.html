Past Projects

Improve robot grasping ability under uncertainties with deep reinforcement learning

Master Thesis

From my previous work, human studies showed that humans performed much better than robots at moving robot fingers while grasping objects under uncertainties. I took one of the found grasping strategies that we learned from human subjects as expert demonstration, and teach robot not only be able to grasp an object at any random in-hand positions, but also capable to grasp a wide variety of objects with different shapes and sizes.

This work is submitted to conference Robotics: Science and Systems (RSS), 2020

Humans and Robot studies on near contact grasping

I designed, and conducted a humans studies on multiple robot hands to observe the grasping strategies that adapted by humans under uncertainties and constrained environments. I concluded an overall 3 primitive control strategies from humans and turned them into fine-tuned PID grasping controllers. This work published to International Conference on Robots and Systems (IROS), 2019.
